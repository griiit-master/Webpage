Privacy Policy

Last Updated: February 10, 2026

1. Introduction

Online Fraud Forensics ("we," "our," or "us") is committed to protecting your privacy and personal information. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you use our fraud recovery and forensic investigation services.

Given the sensitive nature of fraud recovery, we maintain the highest standards of data protection and confidentiality in accordance with international data protection laws including GDPR, CCPA, and other applicable privacy regulations.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Personal Information

When you engage our services, we may collect:

  • Identity Information: Full name, date of birth, government-issued ID, passport details
  • Contact Information: Email address, phone number, physical address, WhatsApp number
  • Financial Information: Bank account details, transaction records, cryptocurrency wallet addresses, payment information related to the fraud case
  • Case Information: Details about the fraud incident, communication with scammers, evidence documentation, police reports
  • Technical Information: IP addresses, device information, browser type, access logs

2.2 Sensitive Information

Special Categories of Data: Due to the nature of fraud cases, we may process sensitive information including financial distress details, psychological impact statements, and in some cases, information about criminal proceedings. We handle all sensitive data with enhanced security measures and only with your explicit consent.

2.3 Information from Third Parties

We may receive information from:

  • Law enforcement agencies and legal authorities
  • Financial institutions and banks
  • Cryptocurrency exchanges and blockchain analysis services
  • Credit bureaus and fraud databases
  • Public records and court documents

3. How We Use Your Information

We use your information for the following purposes:

3.1 Fraud Investigation and Recovery

  • Conducting forensic investigations into fraud incidents
  • Tracing stolen funds through blockchain analysis and financial tracking
  • Identifying perpetrators and gathering evidence
  • Coordinating with law enforcement and legal authorities
  • Pursuing legal action and asset recovery

3.2 Service Delivery

  • Communicating with you about your case status
  • Providing progress updates and reports
  • Processing payments for our services
  • Managing our contractual relationship with you

3.3 Legal Compliance

  • Complying with legal obligations and court orders
  • Reporting suspicious activities to relevant authorities
  • Maintaining records for regulatory purposes
  • Defending legal claims

3.4 Improvement and Analytics

  • Analyzing fraud patterns to improve our services
  • Training our investigators and improving methodologies
  • Enhancing security measures

4. Legal Basis for Processing

We process your personal information based on:

  • Contractual Necessity: To fulfill our service agreement with you
  • Legitimate Interests: To investigate fraud and recover stolen funds
  • Legal Obligation: To comply with laws and cooperate with law enforcement
  • Consent: For processing sensitive personal data, where required by law
  • Vital Interests: To protect you or others from financial harm

5. Information Sharing and Disclosure

5.1 When We Share Information

We may share your information with:

  • Law Enforcement: Police, FBI, Interpol, and other agencies investigating cybercrime
  • Legal Professionals: Attorneys, court officials, and legal advisors working on your case
  • Financial Institutions: Banks, payment processors, and cryptocurrency exchanges to freeze accounts or trace funds
  • Forensic Partners: Trusted third-party investigators and blockchain analysts under strict confidentiality agreements
  • Regulatory Authorities: When required by law or to comply with legal processes

5.2 International Transfers

Fraud investigations often cross international borders. We may transfer your information to other countries where we have investigative partners or where perpetrators are located. We ensure appropriate safeguards are in place including:

  • Standard contractual clauses approved by data protection authorities
  • Adequacy decisions recognizing appropriate data protection levels
  • Specific consent for transfers where required
We Never: Sell your personal information to third parties, share your data for marketing purposes, or disclose case details publicly without your explicit written consent.

6. Data Security

We implement industry-leading security measures to protect your information:

Technical Security

  • 256-bit AES encryption for data storage
  • TLS/SSL encryption for data transmission
  • Secure, access-controlled servers
  • Regular security audits and penetration testing
  • Multi-factor authentication for all systems

Organizational Security

  • Strict confidentiality agreements for all staff
  • Role-based access controls (need-to-know basis)
  • Regular security training for investigators
  • Secure document destruction procedures
  • ISO 27001 Information Security Management certification

Physical Security

  • 24/7 monitored facilities
  • Restricted access to case files
  • Secure storage for physical evidence

7. Data Retention

We retain your information for different periods depending on the purpose:

  • Active Cases: Throughout the investigation and recovery process
  • Closed Cases: 7 years after case closure for legal and audit purposes
  • Legal Proceedings: Until all legal matters are concluded and appeals exhausted
  • Financial Records: As required by tax and financial regulations (typically 7-10 years)
  • Marketing Communications: Until you unsubscribe or request deletion

After retention periods expire, we securely delete or anonymize your information unless we are legally required to retain it longer.

8. Your Rights

Depending on your location, you have the following rights:

8.1 Access and Portability

  • Request a copy of your personal information
  • Receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format
  • Transfer your data to another service provider

8.2 Correction and Deletion

  • Correct inaccurate or incomplete information
  • Request deletion of your data (right to be forgotten)
  • Request restriction of processing in certain circumstances

8.3 Objection and Withdrawal

  • Object to processing based on legitimate interests
  • Withdraw consent where processing is based on consent
  • Opt-out of marketing communications
Important Limitation: Some rights may be limited when we have legal obligations to retain information (e.g., for ongoing investigations, legal proceedings, or regulatory requirements). We will explain any limitations when you exercise your rights.

9. Cookies and Tracking

Our website uses cookies and similar technologies:

Essential Cookies

  • Session management and authentication
  • Security features and fraud prevention
  • Load balancing and performance

Analytics Cookies

  • Understanding how visitors use our site
  • Improving user experience
  • Identifying technical issues

You can control cookies through your browser settings. Note that disabling cookies may affect website functionality.

10. Children's Privacy

Our services are not directed at individuals under 18 years of age. We do not knowingly collect information from minors. If a fraud case involves a minor, we work with parents/guardians and follow additional safeguards for child protection.

11. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy periodically to reflect changes in our practices or legal requirements. We will notify you of material changes by:

  • Posting a notice on our website
  • Sending an email to your registered address
  • Updating the "Last Updated" date

Continued use of our services after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
